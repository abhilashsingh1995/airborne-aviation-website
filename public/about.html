Our story

One captain. One classroom. Fifteen years.

Airborne Aviation Academy was founded in 2010 in Sector-7 Dwarka with a simple promise — that every student would be taught by the founder, not by hired faculty. That has never changed.

A note from your instructor

What sets us apart

Five small choices, repeated for fifteen years.

01 · Cap on size

Twenty-five and no more

We have turned away batches of forty. Twenty-five is the largest room one teacher can actually own. We hold to that.

02 · The same teacher

The captain, every class

Air Navigation on Monday, Meteorology on Tuesday, Tech General on Wednesday — same person. No assistants, no rotating roster.

03 · Exam-backwards plan

Built backwards from DGCA

Our weekly tests mirror the DGCA Pariksha question pattern, not a textbook chapter. Students walk into the real exam knowing exactly how it feels.

04 · Honest counselling

We turn away wrong-fits

If a student's 12th science scores or English fluency suggest CPL is the wrong path, we say so. Sales tactics produce dropouts; honesty produces pilots.

05 · Alumni as proof

Where our students fly

Walk into the academy any morning and you will meet a first officer in IndiGo blue or Akasa red sitting with a current student. That's our advertising.

06 · One location

Sector-7 Dwarka, since day one

No franchise, no expansion drama. Same address, same captain, since 2010 — at Ramphal Chowk, the heart of Delhi NCR's aviation training quarter.
